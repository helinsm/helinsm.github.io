The Method

Five Techniques.
One Calming Reflex.

Newborns are born with a neurological "off switch" that's triggered by womb-like sensations. Chapter 3 teaches you exactly how to activate it — and how to layer all five techniques for maximum effect.

S
Swaddling
Firm, snug wrapping that recreates the contained feeling of the womb. Done correctly, it can calm a crying baby in under a minute.
S
Side/Stomach
For soothing only — this position turns off the startle reflex. Always back-to-sleep for unsupervised rest per AAP guidelines.
S
Shushing
Loud, low-frequency white noise — louder than you think. Inside the womb, blood flow was as loud as a running vacuum 24 hours a day.
S
Swinging
Small, fast, jiggly motion — not slow rocking. This mimics the constant movement babies experienced inside the womb for nine months.
S
Sucking
Pacifier or nursing activates a deep calming response. Layered with the other four, this completes the full soothing sequence.
